The METHOD

The CODE Method™

This is the framework everything inside The Attachment Code is built around. Four stages. Real transformation. No overnight promises — but a clear path forward.

C
Catch the Pattern
Most patterns run on autopilot. This stage is about learning to see yours in real time — before they run you.
O
Observe What's Driving It
Beneath every reaction is a fear, a wound, or an unmet need. This stage helps you look beneath the behaviour and understand what's actually there.
D
Decide a Different Response
This is the pause. The moment between trigger and reaction where a choice becomes possible. You practise it here, so it starts to become real.
E
Embody the New Pattern
Awareness becomes action. You repeat the new response until it stops feeling forced and starts feeling like you. This is where earned secure attachment is built — slowly, consistently, for real.

Questions

Questions

Do I need to know my attachment style to join?
+
No. The assessment is a starting point, not a requirement. If you know your style, the journals and tools will pick up from there. If you don't, the Pattern Mirror can help you begin to understand what keeps showing up in your relationships. You just need to be willing to look honestly. The tools will guide you from there.
Is this only for anxious attachers?
+
No. There are journals and tools for anxious attachers, fearful avoidants, dismissive avoidants, people who are moving toward earned secure attachment, and people who feel like they've lost themselves trying to love someone whose patterns are making the relationship painful. The community includes all styles. Nobody is a villain here. The tools work for anyone who is ready to focus on their own side of the dynamic.
I've already read everything about attachment. Will this add anything?
+
Yes. Because reading about something and practising something different are not the same thing. You can understand every concept in attachment theory and still react the same way when you're triggered. The Attachment Code is built around application, not information. The tools are designed to help you do something different — not just understand why you do what you do.
Is this therapy?
+
No. The Attachment Code is a self-development and educational platform — not therapy, and not a replacement for it. What it does is give you a private, practical space to start doing the work on your own terms. Some people use it alongside therapy. Some use it when therapy isn't accessible right now. Both are completely fine.
My partner is the one with attachment issues. Can this still help me?
+
Yes. And this might be the most important thing to understand: you cannot love someone into healing. You cannot understand them into changing. What you can do is understand your own side of the dynamic — your responses, your patterns, your reactions. When you do that, things shift. Sometimes the relationship improves. Sometimes you gain the clarity to make a different decision. Either way, the growth is yours.
Will this save my relationship?
+
That's not the goal. The goal is to help you understand yourself — your patterns, your reactions, your side of the dynamic. Sometimes that leads to a relationship improving. Sometimes it leads to someone gaining the clarity to move on. The Attachment Code doesn't tell you what to do. It helps you get clear enough to make your own choices, from a place of understanding rather than fear or survival mode.
Why is the founding member price so low?
+
Because the people who need this most should be able to get to it. Therapy costs hundreds. Most free attachment content online keeps you focused on other people's behaviour, not your own patterns. The Attachment Code is priced deliberately so it's accessible. Founding members get that price locked in for as long as they stay. As the platform grows, the price for new members will increase — but not for you.
How long does it take to see a difference?
+
Some things shift quickly. Being able to name what you're feeling instead of drowning in it. Catching the moment before a reaction takes over. Noticing a pattern in the middle of it, not just afterwards. Real change — the kind that rewires the automatic responses that have been running for years — takes longer. There are no overnight promises here. But there is a clear path, and small shifts add up to something significant. The honest answer is: this works in proportion to how consistently you use the tools.
